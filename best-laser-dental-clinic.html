WHEN IS LASER USED IN DENTISTRY

Lasers are basically used as an alternative to the traditional method of dentistry. They are most commonly used for the following cases:

Crown lengthening:
This procedure is performed in cases where the length of the tooth is small due to excess gingival tissue present. It is set right by removing the excess tissue.


Enamel contouring:
The enamel of teeth is set right using lasers by smoothening it out into better shape for improving the overall aesthetic appearance of the teeth.

Gum contouring:
The gingival tissue or the gums is set right by shaping the gums in proportion to the size and shape of the teeth. It also aids in removing the infection or bacteria from the teeth thereby useful in root canal treatments


Tooth decay:
it helps in removing the damaged part of the teeth due to decay without greatly damaging the rest of the teeth in a painless way.

Tooth whitening:
The spots and other damages on the exterior damages on the teeth are removed by using lasers after applying bleaching agents.


ADVANTAGES OF USING LASERS IN DENTISTRY:

It is painless when compared with the traditional method. This helps in alleviating the stress and anxiety levels of the patient.

Bleeding is reduced a great deal as there is no need for sutures or instruments like scalpels

It speeds up the recovery period after procedures

Laser treat only targeted area and does not affect the nearby areas like in the traditional method.
